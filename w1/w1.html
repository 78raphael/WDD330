Video 1

“If you design for the smallest, least powerful device first, then you will focus on the content, and your core functionality. That leads to simple, beautiful apps. If you do it the other way around, it’ll be like trying to put a marshmallow into a piggy bank, which is neither simple, nor beautiful.” – Joel Marsh

Sites have been developed for desktops primarily.

Paradigm Shift:
2007 -> Mobile uprising, exploded around 2011
2011 -> Desktop peak, start of decline of sales

Responsive != Mobile first
Responsive retrofitting: creating a mobile view after a desktop version was already created
Mobile First - Will address constraints of mobile size and bandwidth
Start Small and scale up

Video 2:

Steve Jobs: “Its really heard to design products by focus groups. A lot of times, people don’t know what they want until you show them.”

Be There
Be Useful
Be Quick

Reachability: 75% of users use their thumbs when using their devics
49% use a one-handed grip

Speed Matters:
Google .5 second delay = 20% drop in traffic
Bing 2 second delay = 4% drop in revenue
AOL Fastest 10% of users stay 50% longer

Reduce image use on sites
Use SVG and Webfonts = lower bandwidth
Minify CSS and JS files
Compresss/GZIP to eliminate unnecessary data

Video 5:

“End-users, not technologies, shape the market. Consequently marketers need to stay abreast not only of technological developments but also of the way people respond to them.” – Matt Haig

Mobile Ads were minimal in the 90’s. In the early 2000’s, advertisers saw a great way to increase their visibility. In the 2010’s, ads increased even more, but the space used on mobile devices became used less for content and more for ad space.

Spending has increased for advertising has increased remarkability over the past 5 years.

Making ads nicer:
- Make ads smaller and more simple.
- Don’t make ads full sized to start with. Can become bigger when clicked or when needed. No pop-ups.
- Parallaxed ads help engage the customer more

“The fallen state of our internet is a direct, in unintentional. , consequence of choosing advertising as the default model to support online content and services.” – Ethan Zukerman – Creator of the Pop-up ad

Stop using pop-ups and pop-overs
Use advertising sparingly
Make sure the ads are unobtrusive. Make use of content first.
